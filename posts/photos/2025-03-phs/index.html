Philly Flower Show

March 5, 2025 · 2 min · Jeremy Roschelle

I went to Philadelphia for a conference and wandered into an amazing flower show.

The purpose of my Amtrak trip to Philly was to speak at an AI conference, AAAI. I led a panel on the topic of responsible AI in education. I left DC at 6:00 am and was in Philadelphia by 7:20 am, then walked across town, had breakfast at a classic diner and was on time for the 8:30 am workshop.

At the convention center, I learned that the Philadelphia Flower Show was happening in the other half of the space. So I decided to skip lunch and go see it. All I can say is “Wow!”

(Chick any photo for bigger images; nagivate with arrow keys)

The flower show fully fills an enormous convention space. The first section focused on dramatic displays with many different kinds of cut flowers. These were fantastic. My favorite was a whimsical dinner party set behind the facade of a house. There was also a perfect backyard scene.

Then there were areas for competitions among orchids, cactus, and many other species. The show also had a space for photos and other flower art, for arts and crafts, and for vendor booths. Towards the end, I came across a fun theme of “cactus in a teacup.” It was an amazing experience. Highly recommended. Periodically, judges in yellow jackets walked the floor.

After my conference was over, I met niece Kayla for dinner near the University of Pennsylvania. And the on the Amtrak, back to DC by 9 pm.
