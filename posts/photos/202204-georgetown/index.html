April in Georgetown, a stroll

April 18, 2022 · 1 min · Jeremy Roschelle

Late in the afternoon on Easter Sunday, I wandered through the streets of Georgetown, in Washington DC. It was very quiet. I love how actively people plan beautiful little displays of flowers in Washington DC, especially tulips at this time of year. Many houses have lamps which are lit with actual gas, not electricity. I also wondered along the historical canal that goes a hundred miles or so into Maryland. I’ve really been enjoying the long spring – we started with spring in California in mid-January and now continuing it in DC well into mid-April.
